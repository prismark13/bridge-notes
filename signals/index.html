Signals & Discarding

How you and partner talk through the cards. You play upside-down: a low card means yes, a high card means no. Count and suit-preference fill in the rest.

The four rules

  1. Upside-down attitude — low likes it. A low card encourages the suit; a high card says drop it.
  2. Upside-down count — low-high is even. Your first card low shows an even number; high shows odd.
  3. Suit preference points to a suit. When attitude and count don't apply, a high spot calls for the higher suit, a low spot for the lower.
  4. Discard to keep what matters. Your first discard is a signal; keep length with dummy and never bare an honor.
Which signal, when: partner leads → attitude  ·  declarer leads → count  ·  declarer's first suit at NT → reverse Smith  ·  neither applies → suit preference.

Keep what mattersSafety first
Keep length with dummy so you're not squeezed, hold on to guards (don't bare a king or unguard a queen), and keep parity with declarer's long suit. If a signal would cost a trick or a stopper, skip it — just pitch safe cards.
