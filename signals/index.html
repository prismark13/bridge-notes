Signals & Discarding

How you and partner talk through the cards. You play upside-down: a low card means yes, a high card means no. Count and suit-preference fill in the rest.

the card you play
The four rules
  1. Upside-down attitude — low likes it. A low card encourages; a high card says drop it.
  2. Upside-down count — low-high is even. First card low shows even; high shows odd.
  3. Suit preference points to a suit. High spot calls for the higher suit, low for the lower.
  4. Discard to keep what matters. Your first discard signals; keep length and guards.
Which signal, when: partner leads → attitude  ·  declarer leads → count  ·  declarer's first NT suit → reverse Smith  ·  neither → suit preference
