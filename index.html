Bridge · Defensive Card Play

Second-Hand Play

Second hand low, third hand high. You play before your partner, who plays last — so spend a high card only when playing low would cost a specific trick. The nine counts as an honor throughout.

IA low card is led

Let declarer commit first.

No reason to risePlay low
Dummy ♠ J 7 6   you ♠ K 10 4 — on the 6, play the 4.
Make declarer guess where your king sits; winning now only clears the position for him.
You hold an ace over the leadDuck
Dummy ♥ K 8 6   you ♥ A 10 7 — on declarer's 2, play low.
An ace captures an honor, not a spot card. Grabbing it just promotes dummy's king — wrong about nine times in ten.
Three touching honors, not twoSplit
From ♠ Q J 10 7 play the queen; from ♠ K 10 9 7 the ten — the top of your touching cards.
From only two honors, ♠ Q J 8 3, play low.
Splitting three blocks a cheap finesse of your middle cards; splitting two just hands declarer a tempo.
A concrete reason to winRise
Go up only to: cash a winner about to be ruffed or discarded; deny declarer a trick he couldn't otherwise take; or knock out dummy's last entry to a long suit.

IIAn honor is led toward you

Cover only when it turns one of your smaller cards into a winner.

Cover when

Two honors above the card ledCover, highest
Dummy ♠ J 10 8 6   you ♠ A Q 2 — on the jack, play the ace.
You capture a card that would otherwise score; your queen was dominated by declarer's king anyway.
A lone, unsupported honorCover
Dummy ♠ 10 5 4   you ♠ K 7 2 — cover the ten.
Dummy ♠ Q 4 2   you ♠ K 9 5 — cover the queen, forcing the ace and promoting partner's ten with your nine.
An unsupported honor has no touching card beside it, so covering promotes your smaller cards.

Don't cover when

The honor is supportedDon't cover
Dummy ♠ J 10 8   you ♠ K 2 — the ten sits beside the jack, so covering only makes that ten good. Play low.
Dummy holds a solid runDon't cover
Dummy ♠ Q J 10 9   you ♠ K 8 4 — cover and you turn J-10-9 into three winners for declarer. Duck, and your king takes the third round.
Nothing of yours can promoteDon't cover
Small doubleton under strength: ♦ A K 10 9 over your ♦ J 4 — the jack promotes nothing; play the 4.
Length opposite a short dummy: ♠ Q 6 over your ♠ K 9 5 4 — covering only builds declarer's long suit and can crash partner's honor.
Same when partner is marked short (singleton or void as declarer draws trumps): no card of partner's to promote.

Timing & who leads it

Touching honors led at youCover the last
Dummy ♣ Q J 9   you ♣ K 6 5 — pass the queen, then cover the jack.
Double finesse: ♣ Q J 3 in dummy, your ♣ K 6 2, partner ♣ 10 9 7 5 — let the queen run, cover the jack, and partner's ten is now high.
Cover the first honor instead and dummy's jack simply finesses that ten away.
Declarer (not dummy) leads itRead the dummy
Touching card in dummy — cover: ♠ A J 10 6, your ♠ K 5 2 — on the 9, cover; dummy's ten backs it up.
No touching card — duck: ♠ A 8 6 4, your ♠ K 7 2 — on the 10, play low; declarer holds the jack.

Advanced — the surround play. With an interior holding like K‑10‑9 over dummy's jack, playing the ten rather than low or the king can trap dummy's honor. Situational; count the suit first.
