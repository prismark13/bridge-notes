Third-Hand Play

Third hand high — the mirror of second hand low. Partner has led and plays first; your job is to win the trick or force out declarer's honor, then help partner read the suit.

The four rules

  1. Third hand high. Play your highest to win the trick, or to force out declarer's honor.
  2. Lowest of touching honors. From a sequence, play the cheapest — it tells partner you hold the ones above it.
  3. Finesse against dummy, never partner. If dummy shows an honor and plays low, insert an intermediate; you sit over dummy and capture it later.
  4. Return partner's suit right. Higher of a doubleton, else a low card that shows your length — so partner can count.

When high cards can't help, give an attitude signal — but never signal with a card that could itself win a trick.

IIDummy shows an honor

You sit over dummy, so you can wait and take its honor later.

Dummy has an honor and plays lowFinesse dummy
Dummy ♦ Q 8 7   you ♦ A J 3 — partner leads low, dummy plays low; play the jack, keeping the ace over dummy's queen.
Dummy ♦ K 10 5   you ♦ A J 9 4 — play the 9.
Finesse against dummy's honor only — never insert hoping to trap partner. Your top card stays poised over dummy for later.

IIIReturning partner's suit

Give partner a card they can count.

Which card to returnCount card
From an original four, ♣ K 7 4 2 — return your lowest, the 2 (showing four).
From an original three, ♣ A 8 3 — return the higher of the two you have left, the 8.
The card you return tells partner your original length, so they can place declarer's cards.
Which suit to returnUsually partner's
Return partner's suit unless you can see a faster source of tricks, or partner's suit is clearly dead.
Partner chose the lead for a reason; overriding it needs a concrete plan.

IVReading the hand

Count first, then play as cheaply as the count allows.

Partner leads a length cardRule of 11 / 12 / 10
Dummy ♠ 10 9 8 4   you ♠ A K J — against notrump partner leads the 5 (fourth-best).
11 − 5 = 6 cards above the five are outside partner's hand — you see all six (dummy's 10 9 8, your A K J). Declarer has none, so play the jack; it wins and keeps your A K.
Rule of 11 for a fourth-best lead (notrump). Against a suit you lead third-and-fifth-best, so use the Rule of 12 (third-best) or Rule of 10 (fifth-best) instead.
Doubleton honor over partner's suitUnblock
Partner leads the ♦ K and you hold ♦ A 6 — overtake with the ace and return the 6, so your doubleton doesn't block the run.
Overtake a king with A-x, a queen with K-x, a jack with Q-x — unless dummy has a card that would then be promoted.

The pair. Second hand low, third hand high — two halves of the same idea: let the side that plays last do the deciding. See also Second-Hand Play.
