Second-Hand Play

Second hand low, third hand high. You play before your partner, who plays last — so spend a high card only when playing low would cost a specific trick. The nine counts as an honor throughout.

The four rules

  1. Second hand low. Play your smallest on a low card led; your partner plays last.
  2. Cover to promote. Cover an honor only when it turns one of your small cards into a winner.
  3. Cover the last of touching honors. Duck the first, cover the second.
  4. Split three, never two. From three touching honors play the lowest of your sequence; from only two, play low.

Go up in second seat only for a concrete reason: (a) a sure winner is about to be ruffed or discarded — cash it now; (b) ducking gives declarer a free trick; or (c) rise (often the ace) to knock out dummy's only entry to a long suit.
