Opening Leads

The one card you play blind — before you see dummy. Good leads come from the auction more than a rulebook, but these get you most of the way. Against notrump you attack; against a suit you play safe.

The four rules
  1. Against notrump, lead length. Your longest, strongest suit — fourth-best — or partner's.
  2. Against a suit, lead safe and fast. Sequences and partner's suit are your friends; never underlead an ace.
  3. The card tells the story. Top of a sequence; fourth-best vs notrump, third or fifth best vs a suit.
  4. Read the auction. Lead the unbid suit, avoid the opponents' suits, go passive against a confident auction.
