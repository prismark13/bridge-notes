Opening Leads

The one card you play blind — before you see dummy. Good leads come from the auction more than a rulebook, but these get you most of the way. Against notrump you attack; against a suit you play safe.

The four rules

  1. Against notrump, lead length. Build your longest, strongest suit — fourth-best — or partner's.
  2. Against a suit, lead safe and fast. Sequences and partner's suit are your friends; never underlead an ace.
  3. The card tells the story. Top of a sequence; fourth-best against notrump, third or fifth best against a suit — so partner can read your hand.
  4. Read the auction. Lead the unbid suit, avoid the opponents' suits, and go passive against a confident auction.

IVReading the auction

The bidding is the biggest clue you get — use it.

Choose an unbid suitUnbid suit
After 1♠ – 2♦ – 2♠ – 4♠, hearts and clubs are unbid — prefer one of those over declarer's spades or dummy's diamonds. Leading the opponents' suits usually helps them.
A confident auction, or a slamPassive vs active
When the opponents bid a slam or sound sure of themselves, lead safe — a trump or a sequence — and don't hand over a trick. When you have strength and time, attack your long suit instead.
